# Visual Engine Notes

This document records the current browser-native visual slice for PENUMBRA.

## Current Implementation

- `src/core/visual/renderer.ts` owns the Three.js scene and uses the existing app canvas.
- The globe is a procedural sphere with a low-resolution terrain/bathymetry surface texture derived from the active worldgrid. Ocean color is quantized from bathymetry into discrete blue bands rather than a two-color shallow/deep split, preserving the decomposed grid identity while exposing more physical depth information.
- The surface texture also stores a normalized height value in its alpha channel. The globe shader samples neighboring height values to perturb the lighting normal only, so terrain and bathymetry affect sunlight texture without changing the sphere silhouette.
- Dense generated worldgrids, such as the 5-degree terrain seed, are painted as a restrained land/ocean/elevation texture rather than a global marker field. Small static terrain relief markers are rendered only for sparse fixture-sized grids.
- The restrained visual palette is centralized in `src/core/visual-palette.ts`. Terrain, atmosphere, human presence, quake, precipitation, and UI colors should be tuned there first so layer identity stays coherent.
- The surface texture exists to keep the planet inspectable without adding a secondary playhead or a photorealistic dependency.
- Day/night lighting is shader-driven: the renderer passes a UTC-derived `sunDirection` into the globe shader and computes illumination from the sphere normal. A Three.js `DirectionalLight` exists for scene consistency, but the base globe does not depend on automatic Three.js material lighting.
- Nightlight contacts are visible in production as a restrained night-side human presence field. They are amber emissive surface glows, not point-light objects, and not an extra playhead.
- Sounding nightlight contacts are sampled from the sunrise terminator Gaussian band, so urban light can arrive, pass, and leave before or after the exact centerline reaches the cell.
- Nightlight glow is rendered as one retained `THREE.Points` layer with a custom soft-disc shader. Base presence follows `contactWorldGrid` nightlight cells and is hidden on the sunlit side; contact-local human pulse events temporarily raise the same presence-light mask on both day and night sides so sounding contacts remain inspectable without drawing a separate visual effect.
- Human pulse visibility includes a small core-only floor inside the same presence-light shader. Low-level sounding contacts therefore still emit a minimal inspectable point, while halo size and bloom remain driven by actual pulse strength.
- Nightlight day/night masking for constant presence uses the same Earth-local sun direction as the globe shader. The layer may rotate visually with the globe, but the sunlight test must not use the already-rotated world normal, or daytime human presence leaks onto the lit side.
- Nightlight glow does not rely on depth testing against the globe surface, because screen-space soft discs can otherwise be cropped by nearby surface depth. Instead, the shader fades contacts by their surface normal facing the camera, so back-side glows do not show through the planet.
- Day and night are expressed in a globe shader using the canonical solar state; the sunrise terminator is visible as the lighting boundary, not as an independent global playhead line.
- The day side is intentionally brighter than the night side so the boundary reads as a physical day/night division, not only as a colored scanline.
- The terminator transition uses the scanline sigma as shader softness, so the visual edge reads as a penumbra rather than a hard painted line.
- Production rendering does not draw a separate colored scanline or latitude sample dot chain; the scanline is the day/night transition itself.
- Latitude sample contacts are no longer drawn as an equal-spaced debug dot chain. Debug mode keeps their counts and derived values in the HUD, while visible surface contacts are reserved for local clouds, nightlight, precipitation, and quake contact.
- Terrain colors are aligned to the elevation/bathymetry register bands via `src/core/visual/visual-params.ts`.
- Precipitation, quake pulse, and sounding nightlight pulse visuals are derived from the same `CanonicalScanlineSample` / `AudioFrameParams` objects that drive audio. Constant nightlight presence is visual-only and reads the same `contactWorldGrid` nightlight data directly, gated by UTC-derived sun direction rather than by the Gaussian audio band. Scanline-local cloud samples remain available to audio, rain, and debug state, but are not drawn as a visual point fallback.
- Rain particles in production are driven by the same canonical `water:high` droplet event field used by the Earth texture audio, not by the denser rain granular field. The field is a fixed UTC slot clock with deterministic thinning from current high-droplet density, so browser frame timing, AudioContext start time, and local audio history do not decide which rain drops exist. The renderer turns those events into longer visual tails while the Worklet keeps the existing high-droplet timbre. When the current GFS forecast atlas carries optional `precipitationValues`, only significant PRATE cells are sampled across the sunrise Gaussian band, grouped into stable rain-area candidates, and scattered deterministically from the shared UTC event seed. Without a precipitation atlas, the system falls back to currently known scanline-center weather samples.
- Water low / mid droplet visuals are driven by the same canonical UTC event families as the Earth texture audio: `water:low` and `water:mid`. Each band uses a fixed UTC slot clock with deterministic density thinning. The renderer samples water candidates from the sunrise Gaussian band at the event scheduled UTC, weights low ripples by bathymetric ocean depth and mid ripples by water ratio, then maps accepted events into local surface ripples. Event locations are fixed for the ripple lifetime, so a growing ripple does not hop to a neighboring cell when the live scanline advances. The visible ring is drawn in the globe surface shader from spherical distance, not as a camera-facing point sprite. A separate `waterRatio` mask clips the ripple per fragment, so the trace appears on water surfaces and fades at coastlines instead of crossing land. The rule remains an Earth-layer visual trace, not an added playhead or a new sounding layer.
- Wind texture is visible as a surface particle shimmer inside the same globe shader. It reads the Earth texture wind params derived from `AudioFrameParams`: wind band levels set shimmer strength, wind band Q and roughness set detail/focus, and the renderer continuously integrates a deterministic flow phase. Because the runtime does not currently carry wind direction, the renderer does not draw arrows or directional streamlines and does not claim meteorological wind direction. The visible motion is a longitude-flowing deterministic sparse light-particle field moving opposite Earth rotation; its target speed comes from the wind texture params and is smoothed locally so wind-speed changes do not flip the apparent direction. The large and fine particle fields move at separate fixed multiples of that integrated phase so large particles also read as fast motion. The shimmer band uses a narrow terminator ridge multiplied by a broader Gaussian tail, so the terminator is strongest while the resonance fades out through the penumbra. It should read as a fast surface resonance cue near the terminator, not cloud motion, an additional scanline, a global weather overlay, or a second playhead.
- Cloud audio remains scanline-local. Visual cloud depiction may additionally use a cached `CloudAtlas` artifact as a daytime atmosphere layer. This atlas must be distributed as an app asset rather than fetched per-browser from a point weather API, and must not create an additional playhead or musical driver. The optional precipitation channel in GFS forecast frames is different: it is read only through the sunrise Gaussian band as an Earth-layer rain driver and visual rain placement field. The atlas path is rendered as a transparent cloud shell just above the globe surface, not as terrain color baked into the base globe and not as a full-field point grid.
- Production now defaults to the cached forecast cloud sequence. `?cloud=scanline` explicitly disables the cached cloud atlas and leaves the visual cloud shell empty, while canonical weather defaults continue to feed audio/debug state when no forecast artifact is usable. `?cloud=fixture` loads `/data/fixtures/cloud-atlas.provisional.json` only to test the atlas renderer, and `?cloud=atlas` loads the current cached atlas when present. The default forecast path reads `/data/cloud-atlas.forecast/manifest.json`, reads the referenced forecast frames, and linearly interpolates the two frames surrounding current UTC in the cloud shell shader. The browser periodically refreshes the manifest; if a new sequence appears, the renderer crossfades the old and new sequence textures over the manifest `transitionDurationMinutes` value. Forecast frames are published as versioned URLs before `manifest.json` is atomically replaced, so browsers should not see a new manifest pointing at missing frames; the precompute script keeps the latest 8 frame generations to avoid unbounded artifact growth. The first bridge generators are `npm run precompute:cloud-atlas:openmeteo` and `npm run precompute:cloud-atlas:forecast-openmeteo`, which batch-fetch Open-Meteo `cloud_cover` at a sparse 10-degree anchor grid and resample it into 1-degree visual atlases. The GFS path is available as an artifact-generation route: `npm run inspect:gfs-cloud-source` reads NOAA/NODD GFS `.idx` files and selects `TCDC:entire atmosphere`, `CWAT:entire atmosphere`, and `PRATE:surface` GRIB2 byte ranges, while `npm run precompute:cloud-atlas:forecast-gfs` decodes those ranges with `wgrib2` and publishes the same forecast frame contract. In GFS artifacts, TCDC remains the cloud-cover field, CWAT is normalized into an optional optical-density proxy channel used by the cloud shell, and PRATE is normalized into an optional precipitation activity channel used by the sunrise Gaussian rain band. The generated sequence now covers `f000` through `f015`; after the last frame, runtime allows a 9 hour hold before rejecting the artifact.
- Forecast cloud interpolation and manifest-update crossfade are visual-only. They remove hard jumps between forecast frames and model-cycle updates, but do not create a musical playhead and do not affect scanline-local cloud audio.
- Before publishing a forecast artifact, run `npm run check:cloud-atlas:forecast`. The check validates the manifest and every referenced frame as one sequence, so the shader never has to silently accept missing frames, mismatched timestamps, or mixed grid shapes.
- Cached cloud atlas cover values are not thresholded into a binary cloud mask. The cloud shell uses one cloud color, maps cover through a shaped opacity gate, and, when present, uses the CWAT-derived optical-density proxy to vary opacity and brightness inside saturated 99-100% TCDC regions. Cells whose TCDC cover is essentially saturated keep a small visibility floor even when CWAT is low, so 100% cloud-cover regions do not collapse to only their densest cores. The 1-degree atlas is sampled with nearest-neighbor texture filtering so the decomposed Earth grid remains legible. Brightness is derived from sun-facing direction, limb fade, density, and the final opacity. This keeps color identity stable while avoiding the previous two-color cloud bands.
- In `?debug`, the HUD includes current cloud atlas cover distribution percentiles and the share of cells at or above 95 / 98 / 99 / 100 percent cover. When a GFS frame includes optical density or precipitation activity, the HUD also reports those proxy percentiles plus the current Gaussian-band rain summary so cloud and rain sparsity can be tuned against the source data instead of only by eye.
- Scanline-local cloud point fallback is intentionally hidden in production and debug rendering. When the cached atlas is missing, stale, future-dated, or empty, PENUMBRA shows no visual cloud layer rather than the old dotted scanline-local chain. Production does not call browser-local Open-Meteo by default; `?weather=live` / `?live-weather=1` is the explicit diagnostic route for the legacy weather cache.
- Quake contacts are production-visible only as restrained scanline contacts. Their point opacity and size fade with event age across the 81-minute earthquake window, so they do not read as permanent epicenter markers.
- Quake contact color uses the cyan-blue event palette with a slight terrain/register tint while opacity carries the age fade. Avoid multiplying the color by opacity a second time, or active contacts become invisible even when debug reports them.
- Surface contacts are offset only slightly above the globe to avoid depth fighting. Nightlight glow, quake contacts, and rain particles should read as touching the surface, not as a larger orbital ring around the planet.
- Dynamic point-cloud layers reuse their WebGL geometry/material allocations and update in place for long-running sessions.
- Production HUD text is limited to UTC time, current scanline longitude, and solar declination. Declination is shown as a small seasonal tilt readout, not as a second playhead or control surface.
- Production starts from a minimal entry state: a centered `PENUMBRA` wordmark on the dark field. The wordmark click is only the required Web Audio user gesture; it does not control time or the scanline. The globe, UTC HUD, and audio state appear after the browser-local assets are ready and the audio context has started. Debug, audio-tuning, root-widget, and stream modes bypass this entry state so development diagnostics remain directly accessible. The bundled JetBrains Mono variable font is preloaded, and logo typography remains hidden until the browser confirms that font is ready, then fades in; a short timeout reveals the wordmark if font loading fails.
- `?stream` adds a restrained lower-center `PENUMBRA` title mark set in bundled JetBrains Mono for capture context. It uses the same font-ready gate as the production entry wordmark and is operational typography, not an additional production HUD metric.
- `?capture` is a still-image output mode, not a separate artwork. It keeps the canonical UTC visual render path, skips audio start UI, enables WebGL drawing-buffer preservation, and exposes a small operational control that saves the canvas as a PNG. DOM HUD / panels are not composited into the PNG; the export is the globe canvas only. `capture-scale` may raise the canvas pixel ratio up to 4 for higher-resolution stills.
- `?capture&scene=blue-earth-01` loads the selected 18Z frozen capture scene from `/data/capture-scenes/blue-earth-01/scene.json`. Capture scenes can pin UTC, output size, and a versioned forecast cloud manifest so promotional / goods visuals can be regenerated with the same Earth surface, day/night state, cloud field, and constant night-side human presence. `blue-earth-01-18z`, `blue-earth-01-00z`, and `blue-earth-01-03z` are alternate UTC variants using the same cloud artifact / output settings for visual comparison. In capture mode, the WebGL buffer keeps the requested output aspect ratio and the browser preview is letterboxed/contained instead of stretched to the window. Static night-side human presence scales with capture output resolution so a high-resolution still preserves the same relative night-light visibility as the 1024px preview reference. Dynamic audio-synchronized visual inputs are suppressed: no Human pulse glow, quake pulse, water ripple, or rain particle events are passed to the renderer. This keeps the still image reproducible and avoids freezing a transient musical event as if it were part of the static Earth field. `capture-wind=1` is the exception for visual output: it passes a visual-only `AudioFrameParams` to the renderer so the Earth-layer wind shimmer can be drawn from the same canonical Earth texture params without starting audio. `capture-wind-trail=1` draws the same deterministic particle field as elongated longitude-flowing streaks with a capture-only visibility boost; it does not sample past phases, accumulate browser frame history, or create another playhead.
- Extra runtime diagnostics are only rendered when the app is opened with `?debug`. The debug panel includes selected/candidate human voice counts, maximum current nightlight/music contact gain, a human pulse envelope estimate, water high-droplet density/period, current rain visual point count/source density, and the next UTC nightlight window above the debug threshold, so absent or quiet human musical voices and sparse/dense rain moments can be distinguished from a dark or dry scanline moment.
- `?root=1` is a narrower development inspection mode for mobile and production-like viewport checks. It leaves the full debug panel disabled and shows only the Earth Root / Drone / Earth Beat widget. This flag is not a canonical production HUD addition.

## Boundaries

- Sounding cloud behavior remains local to current scanline samples. A visual-only cached cloud atlas may be used for daytime Earth atmosphere depiction when its provenance is explicit and retrieval is decoupled from viewer count.
- There is no extra orange playhead band in production.
- Nightlight glow does not use real scene lights or whole-screen bloom. The constant component is a night-side human presence field; the reaction component remains local to the sounding contact.
- Quakes are small contact points only; there are no shockwaves, flashes, or event spotlighting.
- Precipitation particles are local to known rainy scanline samples, or to stable rain-area candidates derived from significant cells in the optional GFS precipitation channel across the current sunrise Gaussian band, and are scheduled from the canonical `water:high` droplet UTC event field shared with the Earth texture audio. Water low / mid ripples use the same fixed-clock principle for `water:low` / `water:mid`, but their candidates come from event-time water / bathymetry cells rather than precipitation cells, and their surface shader mask prevents land-crossing ripple marks. They do not create a fourth audio layer or a secondary playhead.
- Wind shimmer is driven by aggregate Earth-layer wind texture params and does not imply local wind direction. It should remain a subtle resonance cue on the surface, not a weather-map overlay.
- `?perf=low`, `?stream`, and `?capture` change operational rendering constraints without changing the canonical visual drivers.
- The renderer does not introduce user-controlled playhead movement.
- The audio layers remain independent; visual contacts do not duck or emphasize other layers.

## Verification

`npm run check:visual` starts from a running local Vite server and uses Playwright Core with local Chrome to verify:

- desktop, mobile, and debug viewports render nonblank canvas pixels with enough contrast for a dark scene
- day and night sides have measurable luminance separation
- the globe is not clipped at the viewport edges
- the production HUD exposes UTC time, scanline longitude, and solar declination
- the debug panel appears only in `?debug`
